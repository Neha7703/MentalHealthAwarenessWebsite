What to do if you are struggling with mental health problems?
Just look after your mental health!
➡ Adopt a healthy lifestyle
Regular exercise, a balanced diet, and a disciplined sleeping schedule can boost your self-esteem and help your concentration. A healthy lifestyle will also increase dopamine production in your brain and make you feel more rewarded for your work.
➡ Take a break
A change of scenery can be enough to de-stress you. Make sure you often take breaks from work or other stressful environments, even if it's only for a few minutes. Listen to your body. Listen to your mind. Sometimes the outside world can wait.
➡ Talk about your feelings
Don't be afraid to talk about your feelings with close family members, friends, or anyone else you prefer. Just being listened to can help you feel more supported. Remember, talking about your feelings is not a sign of weakness but rather a sign of taking charge of your wellbeing.
➡ Ask for help
We all sometimes get tired or overwhelmed by how we feel when things go wrong. Don't be afraid to ask someone for help in these situations. You could join a support group, find a councelor, or visit a guidance practitioner. You could also talk to an emergency hotline.
➡ Stay in touch
Strong family ties and supportive friends can help you deal with the stresses of life by caring for you and keeping you grounded. They can also offer different points of view and help you have perspective over various issues. In essence, they can help you appreciate life more.
➡ Accept who you are
Always remember that you are unique, you are beautiful, and you are valued. Try to learn to feel good in your body rather than wishing to be more like someone else. Accepting who you are is vital for learning new skills, moving on from failures, and being more social.
➡ Drink in moderation
Drinking alcohol is not a safe way to cope with mental health difficulties, as alcohol withdrawal symptoms end up negatively affecting your brain and the rest of your body. Both men and women should stay within the alcohol limits of 14 units per week.

Learn to cope with different forms of mental disorders!
Depression
> Find a routine and stick to it.
> Face your fears.
> Try psychological therapy.
> Don't shy away from taking antidepressants.
> Challenge negative thinking.
> Adopt cognitive behavioral therapy.
Anxiety
> Identify and learn to avoid your triggers.
> Try cognitive behavioral therapy.
> Discover strategies to cope with panic or anxiety attacks.
> Perform some calming breathing exercises.
> Put your anxiety into perspective.
